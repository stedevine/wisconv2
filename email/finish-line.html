We're done!

Last show and EP release on May 11th at LoFi

Press link to the first song : Surrender Dorothy (Soundcloud)

Hello, we're done. We love you. It's over
After creating-genre defining songs and playing bar after bar across the 206 for almost ten years we've decided that it's time to stop.
Yeah. I know
But we're a class act and we're going out with some ceremony. First we've put together an unmissable final show. Check out this line up:
Gibraltar massive indie heroes
Trash Fire young pretenders and your new favorite band
Clutch Douglas! reformed for one night only (because we still have some pull)
We also have Base Tan on the ones and twos. So it's going to be that kind of night
More?
In a move that will surely break us in to the big time - we're releasing our last, best EP "The Finish Line" (produced by Steve Fisk(!)) at this show - come by and pick up an ultra-rare physical copy.
And it's prom-themed, so dress sharp.

Massive thanks to everyone who's supported us, we wouldn't have made it this far without you. Seattle's best joywave band returned to the studio this summer to record six songs that tell a story. They doubled-down on the stacked harmonies, fuzzy distortion and smart keyboard leads that defined their sound and emerged with a stand-out record.
The new EP, Jazz Cigarette, packs these tricks into every gem-perfect song and the listener bounces off the walls as tales of space, video games and sadistic paleontologists blast from the speakers.
Though each song can be taken individually, the recommended dosage is to play the whole record in one go. Maybe late at night. Definitely in the dark.
Wiscon continue their work with indie producer Brandon Eggleston (Mountain Goats, Modest Mouse) who's impeccable ears made every part of this record shine.
Don't sleep on this, at this rate Wiscon won't be Seattle's Best Kept Secret(TM) for long.

Bio

Wiscon is the best band for the beach at and end of the world party. Their bomp sounds like a siren from 60s AM radio fronting a rawking house band revving up the score for a screening of the best sci-fi and horror B-movies. Mash up The Myserians, The Screamers, and The Ronnettes and you got this street level power pop soul fire
